Section01

The work, in four forms.

We do one thing — endodontics — and we do it with care. Below are the procedures that make up the day-to-day, each one geared toward saving the natural tooth.

№ 01
Root Canal
Treatment
Gentle, precise therapy with digital X-rays and microscopic visualization — saving your natural tooth and avoiding extractions.
Read More
A root canal is a common, conservative procedure that removes infected pulp from inside the tooth, then carefully cleans, shapes, and seals the canal. We use:
  • Digital X-rays with significantly less radiation than traditional film
  • Surgical operating microscopes for precision
  • Modern rotary systems that reduce chair time
  • Single-visit treatment when clinically appropriate
Most patients tell us it feels like getting a filling.
№ 02
Endodontic
Retreatment
When a previously treated tooth doesn't heal or develops new issues, our specialists can re-treat it and restore your comfort.
Read More
Even successful root canals can occasionally need follow-up care. Common signs include:
  • Lingering or returning pain
  • Swelling or tenderness near the tooth
  • Sensitivity to pressure or temperature
Dr. Chen and Dr. Vera specialize in retreatment using advanced microscopic visualization — often saving teeth that would otherwise need extraction.
№ 03
Emergency
Root Canal
Severe tooth pain shouldn't wait. We prioritize emergencies and offer same-day treatment when availability allows.
Read More
If pain medication isn't helping and your tooth pain is interfering with daily life, you may need urgent care. We serve Fremont, Milpitas, San Jose, Sunnyvale, Santa Clara, and surrounding Bay Area communities. Get our office numbers →
№ 04
Endodontic
Surgery
When non-surgical treatment isn't enough, microsurgical procedures can save the tooth — including apicoectomy and other targeted root-end surgeries.
Read More
Some teeth need more than a standard or repeat root canal — particularly when infection persists at the root tip, or when anatomy makes conventional access difficult. Endodontic surgery, performed under microscopic visualization, lets us treat the problem directly with minimal disruption. Common procedures include:
  • Apicoectomy (root-end surgery)
  • Root-end resection and filling
  • Surgical management of cracks and perforations
  • Biopsy and removal of pathology
Most surgical visits take about an hour, and recovery is typically faster than patients expect.

Common concerns.

The fastest way to feel better about a root canal is to ask the question that's worrying you.

№ 01Does a root canal hurt?+
No — and we know that's hard to believe. With modern anesthesia and gentle technique, the procedure is comparable to getting a filling. The discomfort you feel beforehand usually goes away by the end of the appointment.
№ 02How long does it take?+
Most root canals are completed in 60–90 minutes in a single visit. Complex cases or retreatments may occasionally need a second appointment, but we'll always tell you up front.
№ 03Do you accept my insurance?+
We are an independent specialty practice and participate as an out-of-network provider. Here's what that means for you:
  • We handle all insurance paperwork and submit claims directly on your behalf — you don't have to do a thing.
  • Your insurance company will reimburse you directly for the covered portion of your treatment.
  • Our team provides a clear cost estimate before your visit so there are no surprises.
  • Being independent means our clinical decisions are guided by your best outcome — not network contracts.
№ 04Do I need a referral?+
No referral required. Many patients are referred by their general dentist, but you're welcome to call us directly. We coordinate with your dentist throughout treatment.
№ 05What does recovery look like?+
Most patients return to normal activity the same day. Tenderness can be expected to last anywhere from a few days, sometimes a few weeks. The vast majority of teeth feel fairly normal by one month. We'll call to check in the day after.
№ 06How much does it cost?+
Cost depends on the tooth and complexity. We provide a written estimate before any treatment and offer flexible financing options including CareCredit. Most insurance plans cover a significant portion.
№ 07Will my tooth need a crown?+
Most molars and premolars need a crown after a root canal to protect the tooth long-term. Your general dentist typically places the crown 2–4 weeks after we finish.

№ Insurance & Payment

Insurance & payment.

Our practice operates independently from insurance networks, which means your treatment plan is guided entirely by what is clinically best for you — never by what an insurance plan dictates. We're happy to help you obtain reimbursement from your provider and supply all documentation needed to file your out-of-network claim.

How it works

A simple, transparent process from your first visit to reimbursement.

01
Verify your benefits

Before your visit, call the member services number on your insurance card and ask specifically about your out-of-network endodontic benefits. Many PPO plans reimburse a meaningful portion of specialist fees.

02
Receive your care

Payment is collected at the time of service. We accept all major credit and debit cards, check, and can discuss financing options if needed.

03
We prepare your claim documents

You'll receive a detailed superbill — an itemized receipt with all procedure codes, diagnoses, and provider information — everything your insurer needs to process the claim.

04
Submit & get reimbursed

You submit the claim directly to your insurer (online, by mail, or through their app). Reimbursement is issued to you based on your plan's out-of-network coverage level.

Payment options

All major credit and debit cards, personal checks, and cash. Flexible financing arrangements may be available — please ask the front desk for details.

PPO Plans Welcome

If you have a PPO plan, you likely have out-of-network benefits. HMO and DHMO plans typically don't include out-of-network coverage — we're happy to clarify before your visit.

What we provide you

A detailed superbill with ADA procedure codes, diagnosis codes, provider NPI, and fee breakdown — fully formatted for insurance submission.

We're here to help

Have questions about your coverage or what to expect? Our front desk is available to walk you through the process and answer questions before your appointment.
